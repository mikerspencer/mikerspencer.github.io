About

Mike Spencer

Mike is a data scientist researching snow hydrology at the University of Edinburgh's School of GeoSciences. His work is based around a historic dataset (Snow Survey of Great Britain) and he has used it to demonstrate a link between snow cover and climate fluctuations and better understand snow cover and melt frequency through computer modelling and statistics. You can read more about Mike's research on his blog, scottishsnow. This research is part of Mike's PhD, which he passed in early 2016.

Prior to Mike's return to university he worked as a hydrologist for Environment Agency Wales and Halcrow Group Ltd. Mike worked on projects including river and coastal flood mapping, flood defence scheme design, river reallignment, flood forecasting (operationally and model development) and flood risk assessment. Mike graduated from Lancaster University in 2004 with a BSc in Environmental Science.

Mike is a member of the British Hydrological Society, Royal Meteorological Society and Royal Statistical Society.

Hydrology

Consulting, research, etc.

Mike has worked as a hydrologist in the public, private and academic sectors since 2005. He has primarily been involved in flood prediction and analysis, but also been part of drought, ecology and water resources projects. When Mike began his PhD in 2011 he diversified his skills and embraced meteorological processes.

GIS

Geographic Information Systems/Software

Since 2005 Mike has used GIS to analyse and manage data and inform decisions. He is a capable user of most GIS products, providing national support for the Environment Agency (2006-2008) and supervising an MSc GIS student.

Programming

Mike primarily works in the R language, which gives him access to a wide library of statistical and data tools. He manages his code using version control (e.g. UK climate analysis) and is a big fan of well documented scripts! Mike uses programming for: data scraping, munging, management, GIS, modelling and all sorts of other things.

Blog

scottishsnow

Mike's scottishsnow blog documents his PhD research findings and tips on handling data and conducting analysis. His most popular posts cover the R programming language, software for open (geo)science and elevation data from the Ordnance Survey. You can read his blog at scottishsnow.wordpress.com.

Fieldwork

Mike has undertaken extensive fieldwork, primarily in cold environments. In 2001 Mike worked a winter season in Northern Alberta in temperatures below -40°C geophysics surveying. In 2005 Mike was largely field based, sampling groundwater from contaminated land across Great Britain. During Spring 2012 Mike was based in Sub-Arctic Sweden collecting data for a biomass, snow and atmosphere interactions project.
